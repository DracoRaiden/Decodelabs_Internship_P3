Under the Hood: The Similarity Engine

Here is how the matching engine processed your query:

Cosine Similarity Formula
cos(theta) = frac{\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}}{\|\mathbf{A}\| \|\mathbf{B}\|} = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^{n} A_i B_i}{\sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} A_i^2} \sqrt{\sum_{i=1}^{n} B_i^2}}$$

Computes the angular alignment between your profile vector $\mathbf{A}$ and the role database vector $\mathbf{B}$, ignoring magnitude skew.

1
Text Cleaning & Tokenization: Your inputs were cleaned, stripped of generic stop words (e.g. developer, system), and split into standard tags.
2
TF-IDF Weighting: Words in your profile and dataset roles were converted into high-dimensional vectors. Rare, highly descriptive words (like kubernetes or pytorch) receive heavier TF-IDF weights, while common terms are penalized.
3
Cosine Score Matrix: The system computed the dot product of your vector and each role's vector, divided by their Euclidean magnitudes.
4
Truncated Filtering: The roles were sorted descending, and cut off at the selected **Top-N** limit to cure choice overload.
